When watching our favorite characters do their thing, it always seems to amaze us the way they interact with others and respond to certain situations. Think back to a time where you fell in love with a character just because of something they said, or how they reacted, you may not have realized it now, but those traits they display are dictated by their personality. It’s essential when writing personality, that you make your character seem human, but first, we are going to need a base personality trait.

But just how does one achieve this?


How can I write an appealing personality for my character?

Start off with a base character trait

The term “base trait”, would refer to how a character appears to others. If they are cocky, timid, happy-go-lucky, or sad, It’s good to establish one to allow your readers to catch a glimpse of your characters self.

Build upon their likes and dislikes

Now that you have established how your character comes off to others, it's time you build a little more. Try honing in on their likeas and dislikes. Do they have a preference for what food they like? Do they like going outside, staying inside? Or playing in the rain? Whatever they like will tell the reader something about themself. If you met a character who liked reading, you may get the impression that they are smart, similarly, even the smallest likes can make a character more human. It's also important to develope on a characters behaviors and how they react to when whatever they like, is taken away from them.

Another thing that could lead a character to have a compelling personality if used correctly, would be their past and how it would affect them.
